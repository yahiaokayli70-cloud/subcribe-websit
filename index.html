what is rickroll

  • A Rickroll is a bait-and-switch meme involving the unexpected appearance of the music video for "Never Gonna Give You Up" (1987) by Rick Astley. It's usually done by disguising the link as something relevant or important, only to surprise the viewer with the song


    • 🕰️ History & Origins

    • 2007: The Rickroll prank began on 4chan, a popular imageboard. Users began posting misleading links that claimed to lead to trailers for highly anticipated content (like Grand Theft Auto IV), but instead redirected to Rick Astley’s music video

    • The term “Rickrolling” evolved from an earlier meme called Duckrolling, where users were tricked into clicking a link that led to a photoshopped image of a duck on wheels

    🚀 Why Did It Blow Up?

    • The song's cheesy ‘80s charm, combined with the element of surprise, made it a viral hit

    • Rick Astley's sincere performance made the prank endearing rather than annoying.

    • Platforms like YouTube, Reddit, and Twitter helped it spread like wildfire.

    🐱 What is Nyan Cat?

    • Nyan Cat is a pixelated animation of a gray cat with a Pop-Tart body, flying through space and leaving a rainbow trail behind it, set to an impossibly catchy Japanese song


    📜 Origin

    • April 2, 2011: The animation was created by artist Chris Torres (aka prguitarman)

    • April 5, 2011: The iconic video was uploaded to YouTube by "saraj00n", syncing the animation with a song called “Nyanyanyanyanyanyanya!” by daniwellP, sung using the Vocaloid voice Utauloid Momo
